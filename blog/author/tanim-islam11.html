Tanim's Stupid Intro

Whatever...

I exist. I am a professional seat-filler. Here are some of my salient characteristics:

  • I breathe oxygen.
  • I am mostly water.
  • Unfortunately, I am ~30% fat by mass...
  • ...but I like good food. I even like bad, fatty food.
  • I have a doctorate. Yay me.
  • I am very snarky about things I hate. Some of my biggest peeves: bureaucracy, traffic, taxes, lack of exercise. I am even snarky about things I love.

In summary, I am very ordinary.

I have a presence in several places online, and I have a home page here. BORING!

This blog, which uses Pelican for static site generation, and the Elegant theme, started off as a conversion of my older Wordpress blog: https://tanimislamblog.wordpress.com. I am transitioning off the old blog in order to document code and mathematical formulae (just look at my philosophy on driving). I also hope to have some "fixed" blog articles (on the media on my Plex server, on the progression of COVID-19 in the United States, etc.) that summarizes changing information.

Airing of grievances part

I have a hate-gasm against people and organizations that describe how well they have documented their tool. The Elegant_ theme is no exception! I started off from this blog post on creating a home page with the Elegant theme, which was necessary but not sufficient.

  • First party foul: Description on how to do this in Markdown only. There was no mention of how to do this in ReStructuredText.

  • Second party foul: I don't believe the folks who made this Pelican theme thought at all how to make an About Me page using ReStructuredText.

  • Third party foul: All the about me crap has to go into the content/pages subdirectory, otherwise no-worky.

In the end, I needed to create a Markdown blog "post" because I could not for the life of me create something intelligible in ReStructuredText. Fuck me!

My attempt to do so, that did not work, is in landing-page-about-me.rst.gz. Pelican has a hate-gasm against things it thinks are blog posts, even if they are placed into one of the static paths.
